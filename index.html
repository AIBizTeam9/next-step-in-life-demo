The verdict

Based on your two futures,
the best next step is
a 90-day creative sabbatical
— not a full year off, not a promotion push.

Neither future is wrong, but both are extreme. Your answers suggest you need reversible rest — a defined pause with a tangible creative project — not an all-or-nothing bet. Take the edge off the burnout, come back with a portfolio, then decide.

The core insight the debate surfaced

You don't want to quit marketing. You want to feel like an artist again.

Path A gave you your creativity back but stressed you about money and gaps. Path B kept your status but hollowed you out. The 90-day version is short enough to be safe, long enough to matter, and gives you a story to tell: "I took a sabbatical and made X."

✦ Your 90-day blueprint

Here's exactly what to do next.

Actionable steps, real links, and a rough budget — pick what fits you.

Week 1 — Talk to your manager

Frame it as a sabbatical, not a quit.

Propose 90 days of unpaid leave + partial remote return. Most marketing roles can be covered by an interim. Template conversation guide and HR policy examples below.

◎ Sabbatical ask script The Muse ◎ HBR: How to take a sabbatical hbr.org ◎ Email template yourdreamproject
Week 2–3 — Book the thing

Chiang Mai or Lisbon. Budget: ~$4,800 for 90 days.

Both have low cost of living, strong creative communities, and reliable wifi. Fly mid-week for 30–40% cheaper fares. Stay in monthly rentals — hostels burn you out.

✈ ICN → CNX flights Google Flights ✈ ICN → LIS flights Google Flights ▭ Monthly stays under $900 Airbnb ◉ Nomad List: Chiang Mai nomadlist.com
Month 1 — Decompress + pick ONE project

Photo series, newsletter, or short film. Only one.

The debate showed your real hunger is creative identity, not rest alone. Make one finishable artifact. Here's where other sabbatical-takers have published theirs.

▨ Are.na — project journaling are.na ✉ Buttondown — start a newsletter buttondown ▣ Behance — publish the work behance
Month 2 — Meet 10 humans in the new field

"Curious coffees" with creative directors, brand founders, editors.

90-day sabbaticals are the cheapest and most legible career-pivot window in your 20s. Use it to get real feedback, not just rest.

Month 3 — Come back with a story

Decide between returning to your role, negotiating a new one, or making the jump.

With a portfolio + 10 conversations + a rested body, the choice will be obvious. Most people in your cohort choose option 2: same company, reshaped role.
